03Proficiency approach

Proficiencies per course, aligned to Perkins V TSA.

Every course on this site lists four to six proficiencies — measurable competencies a student demonstrates, not activities they participate in. The committee’s proposal is to use those proficiencies as the backbone of Perkins V Technical Skill Assessment (TSA) alignment, and as the basis for the Program-of-Study completer requirement. The full thinking is in the POS plan.

Courses marked with a Draft chip are in normal working-document state — the content is accurate, but the teaching team is still polishing wording and proficiency phrasing. Draft is the default while a course is being pair-reviewed; it is not a flag that anything is wrong.

The broader POS work layers industry certifications on top of proficiencies (candidates include CSWA, AWS SENSE, EPA 609, and NCCER Core) so a completer can leave with portable, third-party credentials in addition to local credit.

05POS completer

What a completer needs.

In the committee’s proposal, a Program-of-Study completer demonstrates at least three credits in the pathway, with at least 0.5 credit at the intermediate or advanced level, plus a recorded Work-Based Learning (WBL) experience. The specifics — which courses count toward which pathway, how WBL is documented, and how certifications stack on top — live in the POS plan.

06Work-Based Learning

WBL status, named honestly.

Every POS completer needs a recorded WBL experience. Today, WBL is embedded in some pathways and absent from others. This is a gap we’re working on, not a finished system.

Pathway WBL status Where it lives (or would live)
Woods & Carpentry In place Carpentry runs community builds (Unity Shelter tiny homes) that generate documentable jobsite hours.
Automotive Planned Autos 3 would anchor WBL via a capstone repair or restoration project with documentation.
Vehicular / Drone Planned Capstone-style course includes a build log and run/flight test data — frames cleanly as WBL.
Computer Science Planned Capstone CS final project with public showcase could count, but needs a community or employer partner to qualify as WBL.
Industrial Design Planned Industrial Design II’s self-directed project is designed to connect with a community or client partner.
Metals Planned With Metals III (added April 2026), the AWS SENSE Level 1 capstone fabrication produces a documentable project with weld map + bill of materials. Pairing it with a local shop would close WBL fully.
Engineering Gap Engineering Science is anchored in experiments, not WBL. A future capstone extension is the fix.
Architecture Gap No WBL in the current pipeline. A partnership with a local architecture firm is the obvious move.
Real World Math N/A Credit-bearing support course, not a standalone pathway.

“In place” means students can already document WBL hours today. “Planned” means the course exists and could be shaped toward WBL with modest curriculum work. “Gap” means there’s no WBL-capable course in the pathway and it will need new course design or a cross-pathway solution.

07LBCC College Now articulation

College credit, today vs. planned.

This is the thinnest part of the program and we’re not going to pretend otherwise. Only one course historically carried documented LBCC credit; the 2027+ courses will need re-articulation.

Course LBCC equivalent Credit Status
Intro to Engineering EG 4.407 (historical IDEA articulation) 4 credits Needs re-articulation
Computer Science (Intro / Advanced / Capstone) CS 160 / CS 161 candidates TBD Not yet articulated
Industrial Design I & II MFG / CADD program fit TBD Not yet articulated
Autos 1, 2, 3 AUT program fit TBD Not yet articulated
Metals II WLD / MT program fit (intro level) TBD Not yet articulated
Metals III WLD 121 / 122 candidates (AWS SENSE Level 1) TBD Not yet articulated
Woods II, Carpentry Construction / NCCER path TBD Not yet articulated
Engineering Science, Architecture, Vehicular/Drone Design, and Real World Math have no current LBCC crosswalk identified.

Re-articulation is the ask we carry to LBCC once the course catalog lands for 2027–28. Until then, assume “candidate” rather than “articulated” in any advising conversation.
