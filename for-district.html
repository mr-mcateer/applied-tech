03Proficiency approach

Proficiencies per course, aligned to Perkins V TSA.

Every course on this site lists four to six proficiencies — measurable competencies a student demonstrates, not activities they participate in. The committee’s proposal is to use those proficiencies as the backbone of Perkins V Technical Skill Assessment (TSA) alignment, and as the basis for the Program-of-Study completer requirement. The full thinking is in the POS plan.

Courses marked with a Draft chip are in normal working-document state — the content is accurate, but the teaching team is still polishing wording and proficiency phrasing. Draft is the default while a course is being pair-reviewed; it is not a flag that anything is wrong.

The broader POS work layers industry certifications on top of proficiencies (candidates include CSWA, AWS SENSE, EPA 609, and NCCER Core) so a completer can leave with portable, third-party credentials in addition to local credit.

05POS completer

What a completer needs.

In the committee’s proposal, a Program-of-Study completer demonstrates at least three credits in the pathway, with at least 0.5 credit at the intermediate or advanced level, plus a recorded Work-Based Learning (WBL) experience. The specifics — which courses count toward which pathway, how WBL is documented, and how certifications stack on top — live in the POS plan.
