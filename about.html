00 / About

What this site is, and isn’t.

A living map of the Applied Tech program of study. Drafted by teachers, reviewed by counselors, shared with families and district.

01What this is

A shared reference, not a replacement for talking to people.

What

A map of the Applied Tech program of study.

Nine pathways, five teachers, two buildings — Crescent Valley and Corvallis High. Every course on this site carries CTE credit; two carry dual credit (Engineering Science for science, Real World Math for math).

Why

Because the whiteboard only lives in one room.

Counselors need the whole map without walking to the shop. Families need to see what each pathway actually leads to. District needs dual-credit and Perkins alignment in one place. This site is that place.

Who maintains it

The teachers named on each course.

All content lives in a single file, js/data.js, in the public GitHub repo. Every edit is a commit — so there’s an auditable trail of who changed what.

Who it’s not for

Policy, graduation requirements, or course-change paperwork.

For credit rules beyond CTE, for handbook policy, or to actually change a schedule — talk to a counselor. This site answers “what would I learn?” not “am I allowed to?”

02How to read this site

Four things to know before you click around.

  1. 01 · The freshman row

    The top row is the entry layer.

    Every Applied Tech student takes at least one of Woods 1, Metals 1, or Computer Skills in 9th grade. These are the on-ramps to everything else. You can take all three if you want to try everything.

  2. 02 · Pathway columns

    Each color is a pathway.

    Columns below the freshman row are year-long 10th–12th grade courses, grouped by pathway. The suggested sequence runs top to bottom; dashed connectors are typical paths, not hard prerequisites.

  3. 03 · Credit badges

    CTE is implied. Science and Math are called out.

    Every year-long course on this site carries 1.0 CTE credit, so we don’t badge it. Courses that also carry science credit or math credit show those badges in the header. Today that’s Engineering Science (science) and Real World Math (math).

  4. 04 · Draft vs. reviewed

    A Draft chip means the wording is still being reviewed.

    The content is accurate, but the teaching team hasn’t finished their polish pass. Courses without a draft chip have been teacher-reviewed against department descriptions.

03Credits & data source

One file, five authors, every change a commit.

All course, pathway, and teacher content lives in js/data.js in the applied-tech repository on GitHub. The site has no backend and no database — the data file is the source of truth. To propose a change, open a pull request or email Andy McAteer.

Pathway colors are a visual shorthand and have no authority; the data model carries the meaning. Proficiencies on each course page are drawn from teacher-authored drafts aligned against Perkins V Technical Skill Assessment language — see the district & Perkins page for the full approach.
