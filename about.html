About this site

A shared reference for the Applied Tech program of study at CVHS and CHS, built for counselors, teachers, students, and families.

Who it's for

  • Counselors — use the Pathway Map and the All Courses table to advise students on course selection, credit types, and sequencing.
  • Teachers — share course-level proficiencies and descriptions with your department and committee reviewers.
  • Students & families — start on Explore to see what each pathway leads to.

How to read the map

  • The top row is the 9th-grade entry layer. Every Applied Tech student takes one (or more) of these three courses.
  • Columns below are year-long pathways. The typical sequence within a pathway is shown top-to-bottom.
  • Pipeline note: Only the freshman row is locked as a prerequisite layer. Non-freshman sequences on the whiteboard are suggestions, not hard prerequisites — the committee is still deciding final prereq rules.

Building assignments

CHS hosts: Computer Science (Intro, Advanced, Capstone), Automotive ladder (Autos 1–3), Advanced Metals (Metals II), Vehicular / Drone Design.

CVHS hosts: Intro to Engineering, Engineering Science, Architecture, Industrial Design I & II, Carpentry, Woods II, Real World Math.

Freshman row (Computer Skills, Woods 1, Metals 1) is taught at either building depending on the year.

Credit types

  • Every year-long Applied Tech course carries 1.0 CTE credit.
  • Engineering Science carries a dual CTE + science credit.
  • Real World Math carries a dual CTE + math credit — useful as a second path through the math graduation requirement for hands-on learners.

Status and proposing changes

Courses marked Draft are working drafts — proficiencies and prose will be refined by the teaching team before publication. Courses without a draft tag have been reviewed against existing handbook or department descriptions.

The canonical content lives in a single file, js/data.js, in this site's repository. Propose edits by opening a pull request on GitHub, or contact the Applied Tech teachers directly.
